Peptide Stacking Guide

Evidence-based stacking protocols, synergy principles, and practical combination frameworks for research peptides.

Peptide stacking — the strategic combination of two or more peptides — is widely used in research protocols and clinical settings to achieve synergistic effects across multiple biological pathways. When done thoughtfully, stacking can amplify results while potentially allowing lower doses of individual compounds.

Research Disclaimer: Stacking protocols described here are based on published research, clinical practitioner reports, and community experience. This is educational content — not medical advice. Consult a qualified healthcare professional before using any peptide protocol.

Stacking Principles

Mechanism Complementarity

Combine peptides that work through different pathways. Avoid stacking two compounds that compete for the same receptor, as this can cause desensitization rather than amplification.

Minimal Effective Stack

Start with 2 compounds. Master the basics before adding complexity. Most experienced practitioners recommend stacks of 2–4 peptides maximum.

Timing & Circadian Alignment

Many peptides work best when aligned with natural hormone rhythms. Growth hormone peptides are typically administered before sleep or during fasting windows.

Cycling & Rest Periods

Most peptides work best when cycled (4–8 weeks on, 2–4 weeks off) to prevent receptor desensitization and maintain sensitivity.

Beginner Stacks

Simple 2-compound combinations ideal for those new to peptide protocols.

The Wolverine Stack Recovery

Beginner
BPC-157 + TB-500

Purpose

Comprehensive tissue repair — tendons, ligaments, muscles, and gut healing. BPC-157 drives deep tissue repair while TB-500 enhances cell migration and vascular support.

Typical Protocol

  • BPC-157: 250–500 mcg/day (SubQ near injury site)
  • TB-500: 750 mcg 2x/week (loading) → 750 mcg 1x/week (maintenance)
  • Duration: 4–8 weeks

Synergy Mechanism

BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis and growth factor expression. TB-500 upregulates actin, increasing cellular movement and tissue regeneration. Together they address both vascularization and structural repair.

GH Optimization Stack Growth Hormone

Beginner
CJC-1295 (no DAC) + Ipamorelin

Purpose

Optimize natural growth hormone secretion for recovery, body composition, sleep quality, and anti-aging. The most widely used GH peptide combination.

Typical Protocol

  • CJC-1295: 100 mcg (SubQ, before bed)
  • Ipamorelin: 200 mcg (SubQ, before bed)
  • 5 days on, 2 days off — 8–12 weeks

Synergy Mechanism

CJC-1295 (GHRH analog) provides sustained GH signaling. Ipamorelin (ghrelin receptor agonist) triggers pulsatile GH release. Together they create amplified, natural-pattern GH secretion without cortisol or prolactin spikes.

Gut Healing Stack Gut Health

Beginner
BPC-157 + KPV

Purpose

Address gut inflammation, leaky gut, IBS, and digestive disorders from complementary angles.

Typical Protocol

  • BPC-157: 250–500 mcg/day (oral or SubQ)
  • KPV: 200–500 mcg/day (oral)
  • Duration: 6–8 weeks

Synergy Mechanism

BPC-157 drives gut lining repair and tight junction restoration. KPV (alpha-MSH derivative) dampens inflammatory pathways at the cellular level. Together they repair and calm the GI tract simultaneously.

Interaction Considerations

GH Secretagogues + Insulin

Growth hormone peptides can affect insulin sensitivity. Monitor glucose levels when stacking GH peptides, especially if using multiple secretagogues.

Multiple GHRPs

Avoid combining two ghrelin receptor agonists (e.g., GHRP-2 + GHRP-6) as they compete for the same receptor and can increase cortisol and prolactin.

GLP-1 + GH Peptides

Semaglutide/Tirzepatide reduce appetite significantly. When combined with GH peptides (which can increase hunger), monitor nutritional intake carefully.

Exceeding 4-5 Compounds

Beyond 3–5 peptides simultaneously, it becomes nearly impossible to attribute effects to specific compounds. Keep stacks manageable and trackable.
